# Example: Good Reframe

## User Request
"I need a script to automatically post to LinkedIn every day"

## Office Hours Conversation

**Agent:** Before I build this, help me understand the pain. What's actually happening right now that's frustrating?

**User:** I keep forgetting to post. And when I do remember, I spend 20 minutes writing something mediocre. I know consistency matters but I can't keep up.

**Agent:** Got it. So the real problem isn't "I need automation" — it's "consistent high-quality content without daily effort." What would success look like in 30 days?

**User:** At least 20 posts published, engagement going up, and me not thinking about LinkedIn every morning.

**Agent:** And if we're wrong — if this doesn't work — what's at stake?

**User:** I waste time building something I won't use. Or worse, it posts garbage and damages my reputation.

**Agent:** Here's what I'm hearing: you asked for a posting script, but what you actually need is a **content pipeline** — ideation → drafting → review → scheduling → posting. The automation is the last 10%, not the first.

I'd recommend:
1. **Batch creation** — generate a week of posts in one session (lower daily friction)
2. **Review gate** — nothing posts without approval (protects reputation)
3. **Auto-scheduler** — picks optimal times from a queue (consistency)

The script you asked for is step 3. Want me to build the whole pipeline or just the scheduler?

## Why This Works

- Asked about pain, not features
- Listened for the real problem (consistency + quality, not automation)
- Reframed clearly: "you asked for X, but you need Y"
- Offered options with tradeoffs
- Didn't just build what was asked
