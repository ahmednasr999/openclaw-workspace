# Formatting & Algorithm Rules

## Formatting
- **Short paragraphs.** 1-2 sentences max per paragraph.
- **Line breaks between every paragraph.** White space is engagement.
- **Break at tension points** to force "see more" - never reveal the insight before the click.
- **No hashtags in the body.** 3-5 specific ones at the very bottom.
- **No emojis as bullet points.** One emoji per post max, if any.
- **First line is everything.** It shows in the preview before "...see more"
- **End with a specific question.** "What's your governance model?" beats "What do you think?"
- **Under 1300 characters** for optimal engagement. Over 300 words = you don't have one clear idea.
- **No links in post body.** LinkedIn suppresses external links. Use "link in first comment."
- **No em dashes.** Use commas or hyphens instead.

## LinkedIn Algorithm Levers
- **Dwell time:** Structured content that rewards completion gets boosted
- **Save rate:** Practical, reference-worthy content gets saved. Saves outweigh likes in feed scoring
- **Early velocity:** First 60 minutes determine distribution. Respond to every comment fast and with substance
- **Native content:** No external links in post body (3-5x reach penalty)
- **Posting window:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM audience timezone (GCC = UTC+3/+4)
- **Never post and ghost:** The first 60 minutes is the algorithm's quality test

## Post Types (Rotate Weekly)
- **Story:** Moment, tension, resolution, transferable insight
- **Expertise:** One thing most get wrong, correct model, proof
- **Opinion:** Take, counterargument, evidence, invite
- **Data:** Surprising number, why it matters, one action
