# Interview Frameworks for Candidates (Ahmed)

## SPIN Framework (Reverse-Applied for Candidates)

Use SPIN structure when answering behavioral questions and when asking YOUR questions to interviewers.

### Situation (Context Setting)
When answering: Set the scene briefly (2 sentences max)
- "At Network International, we were managing 15 hospitals across 4 countries..."
- "The PMO had no governance framework when I joined..."

### Problem (Show You Understand Pain)
When answering: Show you recognized the problem before being told
- "The challenge was that each country had its own processes, creating 4x the coordination overhead..."
When asking: Surface THEIR problems
- "What's the biggest coordination challenge across your business units right now?"
- "Where does the current governance model break down?"

### Implication (Demonstrate Strategic Thinking)
When answering: Show you understood the downstream impact
- "Without standardization, we were losing 3 months per project to rework alone..."
When asking: Help them feel the cost of inaction
- "How does that fragmentation affect your ability to hit the Q4 targets?"
- "What happens to team retention when that pattern continues?"

### Need-Payoff (Position Yourself as the Solution)
When answering: Let results speak
- "After implementing the unified governance framework, project delivery improved 40%..."
When asking: Let them articulate why they need YOU
- "If that coordination problem was solved, what would that unlock for the team?"
- "What would it mean for the business if you had one person owning the cross-functional alignment?"

## Gap Selling (Reverse-Applied)

Position yourself as the bridge between their CURRENT STATE and FUTURE STATE.

### Map Their Current State (Research Before Interview)
- What's broken in their org? (Check Glassdoor, news, job posting language)
- What technology gaps exist? (Job description clues)
- What's their competitive pressure?
- Why is this role open? (Growth vs replacement vs transformation)

### Paint Their Future State (In Your Answers)
- "Based on what you've described, it sounds like you need someone who can..."
- "The gap between where you are and where you want to be is exactly the kind of challenge I solved at [company]..."

### Quantify THE GAP (Make the Cost of Not Hiring You Visible)
- "Every month without unified PMO governance costs roughly X in duplicated effort..."
- "The 8-country rollout I led at NI faced the exact same gap - here's how we closed it..."

## Questions Ahmed Should ALWAYS Ask

1. "What does success look like in the first 90 days?" (Reveals priorities)
2. "What's the biggest challenge the team is facing that this role is expected to solve?" (Reveals pain)
3. "How does this role connect to the company's strategic priorities for the next 12 months?" (Shows strategic thinking)
4. "What happened with the person who was in this role before?" (Reveals context)
5. "What's the decision-making process for this hire?" (Shows executive awareness)
