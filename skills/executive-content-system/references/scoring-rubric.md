# Executive LinkedIn Scoring Rubric

Score each dimension 1-10. Total 50.

## 1. Hook strength

10: Specific, credible, scroll-stopping, directly relevant to GCC/executive audience.
7: Clear and interesting but could be sharper.
4: Generic or familiar.
1: Vague, clickbait, or misleading.

Look for numbers, operational tension, contrarian insight, or a concrete executive problem.

## 2. Executive credibility

10: Sounds like a senior operator with real delivery judgment.
7: Professional and credible, but light on proof.
4: Generic consultant tone.
1: Hype, motivational fluff, or unsupported authority.

Never reward invented claims.

## 3. Value density

10: Reader leaves with a usable rule, framework, lens, or decision.
7: Useful but could be more concrete.
4: Mostly opinion with little application.
1: Empty or obvious.

## 4. Voice fit

10: Direct, practical, warm but sharp, aligned with Ahmed's executive style.
7: Mostly aligned with minor generic phrases.
4: Noticeably AI-written or creator-generic.
1: Wrong persona.

Ahmed's posts should avoid em dashes, generic motivational phrasing, and broad claims with no operational detail.

## 5. Publish readiness

10: Ready after final proofread, with visual decision clear.
7: Needs small edits.
4: Needs evidence, restructuring, or visual work.
1: Should not publish.

Blockers:
- unsupported factual claim
- missing expected image
- no CTA/question when appropriate
- wrong audience
- invented achievement or metric
