# Pattern: Section Divider

## Best for
- signaling a major transition
- resetting rhythm between dense sections

## Use when
- starting a major section
- the audience needs a clear transition marker

## Don’t use when
- the section is too short to justify a divider
- the slide is trying to carry substantive detail too

## Required content inputs
- section title

## Optional content inputs
- section number
- one-line setup statement

## Layout logic
- minimal composition
- one large section identifier or title block
- generous whitespace
- visually distinct from content slides

## Hierarchy rules
- section number or title should dominate
- optional setup text must stay secondary

## Focal-point rule
The slide should have one dominant transition marker, not multiple decorative devices.

## Visual treatment guidance
- allow bolder contrast than normal content slides
- treat this like a pause or chapter page
- keep content extremely restrained

## Common anti-patterns
- turning a divider into a content slide
- too many ornaments
- divider that looks visually identical to normal slides

## Example structures
- large numeral + title
- oversized title band
- typographic chapter page

## QA checklist
- does this feel like a reset?
- is the divider cleaner and bolder than nearby content slides?
- is the content restrained enough?
