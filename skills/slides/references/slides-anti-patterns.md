# Slides Anti-Patterns

Load this file during planning and again during final QA.

Its purpose is to catch template-looking output before delivery.

---

## 1. Structural anti-patterns

### Title plus bullets on white
If a slide is just a heading and bullets with no visual composition, it is a failure unless the user explicitly asked for minimal utility slides.

### Equal-weight card grids
Rows of identical cards flatten hierarchy and make the deck feel generic.

### Dashboard creep
Too many small panels, labels, mini-charts, and outlined boxes make a deck feel operational, not editorial.

### Repeated layout reuse
If 2 to 3 adjacent slides use the same arrangement, the deck rhythm is collapsing.

---

## 2. Visual anti-patterns

### Accent everywhere
If everything important is accented, nothing is focal.

### Heavy shadows and gloss
Avoid shadow-heavy cards, glossy treatments, glow, and decorative polish that does not improve hierarchy.

### Decorative icons without information value
Icons should clarify, not decorate empty space.

### Over-boxing
If every content block gets a visible container, the slide starts to feel like software UI or a business template when it may not need to.

---

## 3. Hierarchy anti-patterns

### Weak focal point
If the slide has no dominant element, it will feel flat even if technically correct.

### Similar text scales
If title, subheading, and body sizes are too close, the slide has no hierarchy.

### Too much bold in body copy
Bold body text quickly turns into noise. Reserve weight for titles, section labels, and rare emphasis.

### Symmetry without need
Balanced two-column layouts are often too safe unless the slide is a genuine comparison.

---

## 4. Content anti-patterns

### Too many ideas on one slide
If a slide tries to explain, compare, justify, and summarize at once, split it.

### Chart without art direction
Dropping a chart onto the page without giving it hierarchy, framing, or takeaway language is not enough.

### Summary as bullet dump
Executive summary slides should emphasize one thesis, not just restate all points equally.

---

## 5. QA questions

Ask before delivery:
- what is the focal point?
- what can be removed?
- does this slide look system-designed or template-filled?
- is the accent restrained?
- is the composition stronger than a default two-column slide?
- does the slide still work if one decorative element is deleted?

If the answer trends toward neat, safe, or generic, redesign before delivery.
