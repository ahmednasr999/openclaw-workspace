# Pattern: Agenda

## Best for
- showing section structure
- orienting the audience early in the deck

## Use when
- the presentation benefits from a clear roadmap
- there are 3 to 6 meaningful sections to preview

## Don’t use when
- the deck is too short to need explicit navigation
- the agenda would just duplicate obvious section names with no value

## Required content inputs
- 3 to 6 agenda items

## Optional content inputs
- short descriptor per item
- section numbering

## Layout logic
- prioritize scannability over decoration
- give one item slight emphasis only if needed
- maintain clean vertical or modular rhythm

## Hierarchy rules
- slide title introduces navigation
- agenda items should scan quickly
- descriptions are optional and must remain brief

## Focal-point rule
The focal point is the structure itself, not a decorative flourish. The agenda should feel crisp and intentional.

## Visual treatment guidance
- use numbering, spacing, or structure as the visual engine
- do not let the agenda become a row of generic cards unless modularity truly helps

## Common anti-patterns
- too many items
- equal decorative emphasis on every item
- long descriptions that make scanning slow
- agenda slide that looks like a dashboard

## Example structures
- vertical numbered list
- two-column roadmap
- restrained navigation bands

## QA checklist
- can the viewer scan all items in 2 to 3 seconds?
- is the structure clean?
- does the page feel editorial rather than administrative?
