# SGH HIS Adoption Readiness Report, Evaluation
Date: 2026-03-02
Prepared for: Ahmed Nasr

## Executive Summary
The current report is a strong diagnostic starting point, but it is not yet decision-grade for steering committee execution. It identifies key adoption risks clearly, but lacks operational control elements such as quantified baselines, owners, deadlines, prioritization logic, and go-live gates.

Verdict: Good draft for diagnosis, not yet fit for execution governance.

## Strengths
1. Uses an adoption lens, not only technical readiness.
2. Identifies critical risk categories clearly: training, communication, alignment, engagement, capability.
3. Includes practical mitigation direction for each issue.
4. Uses structured observation methodology, stronger than perception-only survey input.

## Gaps That Reduce Decision Quality
1. Missing score evidence in the body:
   - No dimension scores by site, department, or role group.
   - No trend or variance visibility.

2. No execution ownership:
   - Recommendations have no owner, due date, or status.
   - Not operationally trackable.

3. No prioritization model:
   - No severity x likelihood assessment.
   - No impact-on-go-live ranking.

4. No go-live control gates:
   - No definition of blocker versus non-blocker risks.
   - No minimum readiness thresholds by function.

5. No KPI framework:
   - No weekly indicators to track mitigation progress.
   - No early-warning adoption metrics.

6. Language quality and structure:
   - Repetition and grammar inconsistencies reduce executive clarity and confidence.

## Required Upgrades Before Steering Use
1. Add a top risk register with fields:
   - Risk ID, risk statement, root cause, impact, owner, due date, status, mitigation action, residual risk.

2. Add RAG readiness by department/site.

3. Add go-live gate criteria with pass or fail thresholds.

4. Add a 2-week mitigation sprint plan.

5. Add weekly adoption KPI dashboard:
   - Super-user readiness rate
   - Workflow sign-off completeness
   - Training readiness by role
   - Resistance incident count
   - Cross-functional escalation age

## PMO Recommendation
Convert the report into a governance-ready risk control pack and use it as the single source for go-live readiness decisions.

## Immediate Next Action
Reissue the report in a standardized structure with quantified readiness, accountable owners, and decision gates.
