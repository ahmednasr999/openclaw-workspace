## Daniel Priestley's Everyday CTA Strategy ($7,000/Day LinkedIn Revenue) | 2026-02-25
**Source:** https://youtu.be/tuZ3tSDxLQM (ScoreApp YouTube)
**Category:** content-strategy
**Key Insights:**

### The Core Framework: Everyday CTA
Every LinkedIn post has TWO parts, completely independent of each other:
1. **Part 1: Value** (something worth reading)
2. **Part 2: Call to Action** (completely unrelated to the value, just "by the way...")

### Part 1: The 3S's and 2F's (What Gets Cut-Through)
Five content hooks that always work:
1. **Scary** - Trends that could disrupt the industry, alarming news, threats
2. **Strange** - Something no one else is saying, quirky angle, unexpected take
3. **Sexy** - Desirable outcomes (how to make $1M, win awards, grow faster than anyone)
4. **Familiar** - Discuss familiar people/topics (e.g., "3 secrets from Warren Buffett")
5. **Free Value** - Give away something that seems like it should cost money (research, frameworks, data)

### Content Formats That Work
- 100-500 word text posts
- Images or carousels
- Diagrams and visualized data (donut charts, maps with percentages)
- Video (LinkedIn loves video, sometimes outperforms YouTube)
- Videos up to 15 minutes work on LinkedIn

### Part 2: The CTA (Rules It Breaks)

**Rule broken #1: Value and CTA don't need to be connected.**
- Use "by the way..." as a gear change
- Completely separate topic from the value content
- This REMOVES call-to-action anxiety (CTAA)
- When they were linked, it felt needy. Unlinked feels natural.

**Rule broken #2: Links directly in posts work fine.**
- Multiple experts say "never put links in posts"
- Priestley tested: posts with links vs without get same views, engagement, shares
- LinkedIn is NOT penalizing links in posts (as of 2025-2026)

**Rule broken #3: Daily promotion doesn't cause blowback.**
- Posted every day for 2 years with a CTA, zero complaints
- "With or without you" energy: the offer is available, take it or leave it
- No one called him a shameless self-promoter

### Four CTA Types to Rotate
1. Assessment (scorecard, quiz)
2. Waitlist
3. Webinar
4. Mini-course

### Key Stats
- $7,840 average new revenue per post
- $50,000+ per week from daily posting
- Some posts: only 40 likes but 200 link clicks (lurkers click without engaging)
- Working consistently for 2+ years

### Content Calendar Advice
- Plan 20 days of rolling content
- Use AI (Claude/ChatGPT) to help plan
- Half a day to map out 20 days
- Keep a reservoir/bucket of content ready
- When industry news breaks, it takes precedent

### Landing Page Requirements
- Hook
- Value proposition
- Credibility
- Clear call to action

**How this connects to Ahmed's goals:**

- Each post: share transformation/leadership insight (3S's + 2F's) + unrelated CTA
- CTA could be: "By the way, I'm exploring VP/C-suite opportunities in Dubai. If your network is hiring digital transformation leaders, here's my profile."
- Or link to a portfolio page, assessment, or calendar booking
- The "value doesn't need to link to CTA" insight removes the biggest barrier to consistent posting

## Lara Costa's LinkedIn Personal Brand Playbook (via Liam Ottley) | 2026-02-25
**Source:** https://youtu.be/3iR3kHxCwfo (Liam Ottley YouTube)
**Category:** content-strategy

### Core Thesis
LinkedIn is still the #1 platform for B2B leads (80% of all B2B leads). You don't need volume, you need quality. 4 posts/week beats daily posting if done right. Lara went from 0 to 300,000 followers posting 4x/week for 3 years. No viral videos needed, just writing.

### Two Pillars of Personal Branding
The 0.1% do BOTH:
1. **Storytelling** - Retain people through narrative
2. **Education** - Teach people through value

Stories so dense in education that people think they're learning but stay because of the narrative.

### Finding Your Unfair Advantage
Two frameworks:

**1. Monetizable Expertise**
- Knowledge that directly changes someone's outcome in a way they'll pay for
- Lead with "how I" not "how to" (credibility through personal experience)
- Be specific: numbers, metrics, timelines
- Example: "Here's the exact lead gen strategy I'm using" beats "Here's how to get leads on LinkedIn"

**2. Strategic Arbitrage**
- Take what's trending/working and use YOUR story or skill through that lens
- LinkedIn users want: how to make more money, make parents proud, achieve dreams, specific career outcomes
- Tap into trends EARLY (LinkedIn gets news a week later than Twitter, easy to ride waves)
- Example: Jake saw Reddit stock news on Twitter, posted about it on LinkedIn, got 2,000 likes after 3 weeks dormant

### The 3 Content Pillars (4-3-2-1 Framework)
4 posts per week across 3 types:
1. **Growth Content** - Niche specific (AI for roofing businesses)
2. **Total Addressable Market (TAM) Content** - Broad industry topics (general AI trends) that funnel qualified leads
3. **Sales Content** - Closes them

Loop consistently: TAM brings eyeballs, Growth qualifies them, Sales converts them.

### Hook Writing Rules
- First two lines: MAX 8 words each (that's the mobile cutoff before "see more")
- Second line = "rehook" (second chance to retain, as compelling as the first)
- Use specific numbers and metrics in rehook
- Don't be mysterious ("here's 10 ways..." is dead)
- Give everything away at the start

### The SLAY Framework (Post Structure)
- **S** - Start with a Story
- **L** - Lead with a Lesson
- **A** - Actionable Advice (steps, breakdown)
- **Y** - End with You (feel-good closer + CTA or call to engage)

### The Art of the Humble Brag
- Never: "I just became a millionaire. Congratulate me."
- Instead: "POV: you became the first millionaire in your family" + photos of proud dad
- Make it about THEM through YOUR story
- Stories are never about you, always about them
- Photos are essential. Story without a photo doesn't hit the same.

### Broad-Narrow-Niche Framework (Post Structure)
- **Broad hook**: "My one-person business is about to cross $200K/month" (attracts wide audience)
- **Narrow problem**: "With zero outbound, zero cold emails" (filters to interested people)
- **Niche solution**: Detailed breakdown of exact strategy (converts followers)

### Authority Jacking
- Use photos with influential people for halo effect
- Gets you 50,000 followers in 6 months
- If they engage/like your post, 10% of their audience sees it
- WARNING: Don't tag people who won't respond. LinkedIn marks it as spam.
- If you can't text them to ask for a like, don't tag them

### LinkedIn Algorithm Insights
- LinkedIn PUSHES your first few posts to keep you on the platform
- Quality > Volume (4x/week beats daily low-quality)
- Decision makers are nearly guaranteed to see your content
- LinkedIn is a networking platform: more connections = better performance
- Photos dramatically increase engagement
- You can combine content types (educational + storytelling, educational + authority jacking)

### Story Mining Methods
- Weekly self-interview: what happened, wins, new clients, speaking events
- Voice notes to ChatGPT throughout the week, get weekly summary
- AI call recorders on client calls: problems solved = content ideas
- Take photos constantly (you'll need them for posts)

### AI Usage (Right vs Wrong)
- ChatGPT alone = average output = AI slop
- AI slop IS your competition (and it's bad, so easy to beat)
- Use AI for: generating hooks, content ideas, summarizing call transcripts
- Train AI with your frameworks, voice, and style
- Problem isn't speed of content, it's specificity and clarity

### SLAY Framework (Detailed Breakdown)
The core viral writing structure:

| Letter | Purpose | Marketing Function |
|---|---|---|
| **S** - Story | Get attention by being engaging | ATTENTION |
| **L** - Lesson | Retain them with insight | RETENTION |
| **A** - Actionable Advice | Give tactical value people save | CONVERSION |
| **Y** - You (point back to reader) | Ask a question, make it about them | ENGAGEMENT |

- Story gets attention
- Lesson gets retention
- Actionable advice gets conversion
- "You" gets saves, comments, shares
- Used by 10,000+ people
- Works because it follows fundamental attention > retention > conversion flow

### Content Pillar Mix (4-3-2-1 Expanded)
- **Growth content**: Niche-specific, targets your exact buyer
- **TAM content**: Broad industry topics that attract wider audience into your funnel
- **Sales content**: Directly promotes your offer/service
- Trend jacking works as a bonus growth accelerator on top of these three
- Combine types freely: educational + storytelling, educational + authority jacking, trend + education + sales

### Real Example: Authority Jacking + Trend Jacking
- Grace Beverly post: documented losses, media tearing her apart, told as a story = 2,000 likes (her average is 100-200)
- Proves: adversity stories + familiar figures = massive engagement spikes

### SLAY Framework: Execution Details

**Story (S):** Don't start with the lesson. Start with a hook that makes people react.
- Example: "I've read many books but this one changed my life" > "oh which one?" > "NOT Rich Dad Poor Dad" = reaction
- The second line (rehook) is almost as important as the first
- Bad: "Here's 6 principles to win on LinkedIn" (no one cares)
- Good: Story first, THEN "here's the 6 principles" (they care because you earned it)

**Lesson (L):** Keep each line to ~8 words. Concise = easy education = immediately applicable.
- Goal: every post, reader implements ONE thing, gets results, comes back
- "TikTokification of LinkedIn content": make it skimmable and snackable
- Don't write essays. Same logic as a 3-second viral video.
- Lines that wrap around and look messy = death

**Actionable (A):** Lay out steps as simply as possible. Numbered lists. Short bullets.

**You (Y):** End with a feel-good cliche quote. Why?
- People never remember the start, they always remember the end
- If they feel good at the end, they scroll BACK to the start
- LinkedIn ranks content by TIME SPENT on post (not just likes)
- More time on post = more impressions
- Save + comment = algorithm boost

### PAS Framework (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
Second writing framework, simpler than SLAY:
- **Problem**: State their pain. "Founders, your LinkedIn content sucks."
- **Agitate**: Make it urgent. "It's costing you thousands of dollars daily."
- **Solution**: Be the answer. "Here's how to write content that sells."

Works especially well for:
- Trend jacking ("SEO is dead" style posts)
- Scare + educate + sell pattern
- Shorter, punchier posts vs SLAY's story-driven approach

### LinkedIn Algorithm Insight (Critical)
- LinkedIn ranks content by **dwell time** (how long people stay on post)
- Impressions increase when retention increases
- Saves and comments boost ranking
- Strategy: retain people as long as possible, make them feel good at the end so they re-read

### LinkedIn Post Checklist (Lara's Final Rules)
1. **8-word hooks** (mobile cutoff, maximum impact)
2. **Specific outcome or number** in the first 2 lines always
3. **Varied sentence lengths** (formatting psychology):
   - One line, space, one line, space, one line, space
   - Then shift to: line, line, line (no spaces)
   - Follows F-shape reading pattern (same as landing page design)
   - People get the most value without reading the whole thing
4. **Direct step-by-step OR clear story outcome** in the body
5. **Clear CTA** at the end (every time)

### Content Creation Philosophy

- Save a swipe file of viral posts you admire

- You don't need to be super original. You need to do what works.
- Upload all your existing content (podcasts, SOPs, docs) as a knowledge base for AI to draw from
