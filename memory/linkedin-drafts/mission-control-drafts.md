## Post 1: The #1 Mistake Project Managers Make

**Status:** Review
**Priority:** High
**Platform:** LinkedIn
**Created:** 2026-02-19

**Body:**

They confuse activity with progress.

Every status meeting filled with updates. Every dashboard showing green. Every team member "busy."

But the project is dying.

Here's what I learned after 20+ years leading enterprise programs:

1. **Track outcomes, not tasks**
   - "We completed 47 tickets" means nothing
   - "We reduced patient wait times by 30%" means everything

2. **Kill the weekly status report**
   - Replace it with: What decision do you need from me?
   - Watch how fast things move

3. **Your PMO is a strategic function, not a reporting factory**
   - If your PMO only produces slides, you have a presentation team
   - A real PMO connects execution to strategy

4. **The best PMs say NO more than YES**
   - Every yes without a no is a lie about capacity
   - Protect your team's focus like it's your job (it is)

5. **Stakeholder management > project management**
   - The project plan is 20% of success
   - Alignment, trust, and communication is the other 80%

Stop managing projects. Start leading outcomes.

What's the hardest lesson you've learned in project management?
