# Interview Prep Protocol

*Standardized approach for any executive interview*
*Last updated: 2026-02-21*

---

## Pre-Interview Checklist (Start 24-48 hours before)

### Company Research
- [ ] Company history and founding story
- [ ] Recent news (last 6 months)
- [ ] Financial performance / funding round
- [ ] Leadership team (CEO, CTO, board)
- [ ] Products and services
- [ ] Market position and competitors
- [ ] Culture and values
- [ ] Size (employees, offices, geographic spread)
- [ ] Recent acquisitions or pivots

### Role Research
- [ ] Exact job description (re-read)
- [ ] Reporting structure (who do you report to?)
- [ ] Team size and composition
- [ ] Key priorities for first 90 days
- [ ] Success metrics for the role
- [ ] Budget authority
- [ ] Biggest challenge facing the role

### Interviewer Research
- [ ] LinkedIn profile
- [ ] Recent posts or speaking
- [ ] Background and tenure
- [ ] Priorities and focus areas

## STAR Stories Framework

Prepare 5-7 stories covering:

### Leadership & Team Management
- Led team through transformation
- Managed conflict or difficult situation
- Built team from scratch / scaled team

### Budget & P&L
- Managed large budget
- Cut costs while maintaining quality
- Justified investment to leadership

### Cross-Functional Collaboration
- Worked with C-suite
- Drove alignment across departments
- Handled stakeholder conflict

### Problem Solving
- Turned around failing project
- Made hard decision with tradeoffs
- Data-driven decision that worked

### Delivery & Scale
- Delivered on tight deadline
- Scaled operations dramatically
- Launched new product / service

**Story Structure:**
1. **Situation** — Context (1 sentence)
2. **Task** — Your responsibility (1 sentence)
3. **Action** — What you did specifically (3-5 sentences, use "I" not "we")
4. **Result** — Quantified outcome (1-2 sentences, with numbers)

## Questions to Ask Interviewer

### About the Role
1. What does success look like in this role at 30/60/90 days?
2. What are the top 3 priorities I should focus on in the first quarter?
3. What challenges is the team/company facing right now that this role will solve?
4. How do you measure performance in this position?
5. Who will I be working most closely with?

### About the Team
6. Can you tell me about the team I'll be managing?
7. What's the team's biggest achievement in the last year?
8. What skills or experience would make someone a great fit?

### About the Company
9. How has the company changed in the last 2 years?
10. What's the company's biggest opportunity right now?
11. How would you describe the company culture?

### About Growth
12. What's the career path for someone in this role?
13. What learning opportunities exist?

### Closing
14. What's the next step in the process?
15. Is there anything else you'd like to know about my background?

## Salary Negotiation Framework

### Know Before You Walk In
- **Walkaway number** — Minimum you'll accept
- **Target number** — Ideal offer
- **BATNA** — Best alternative (other opportunities, current job)

### When to Discuss Salary
- **Never** give a number first
- If asked: "What's your expectation?" → "I'd love to hear more about the role and responsibilities first. What's the budget for this position?"
- If pressed: "Based on my research and the scope described, I'm targeting [target range]."

### Timing
- Wait for offer before negotiating
- Express enthusiasm first, then discuss
- Never negotiate via email — ask for a call

### Leverage Points
- Multiple offers (even verbal)
- Unique skills or experience
- Market data (salary benchmarks)
- Start date flexibility
- Non-salary components (equity, bonus, benefits)

### Negotiation Script
1. "Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about the opportunity."
2. "After considering the overall package, I'd like to discuss [salary / equity / bonus]."
3. "Based on my research and the scope of this role, I was hoping for [target + 10-15%]."
4. [Listen — let them respond]
5. "I understand. What flexibility do you have on [component]?"
6. "I'm looking for a commitment of [walkaway]. Can you make that work?"

## Post-Interview Protocol

### Within 24 Hours
- [ ] Send thank-you email to each interviewer
- [ ] Note any follow-up items promised
- [ ] Update pipeline status

### If No Response (7-10 days)
- [ ] Follow up politely
- [ ] Express continued interest
- [ ] Ask if additional information needed
