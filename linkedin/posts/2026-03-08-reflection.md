Sunday Thought.

I used to think the biggest risk in transformation was technology failure.

Wrong. The biggest risk is moving fast on the wrong assumption and not realizing it until you've built a floor on a cracked foundation. I've seen this across healthcare, fintech, e-commerce. The pattern is always the same: strong execution, wrong premise.

This week I'm asking myself one question before every decision: "What would have to be true for this to fail?" Not to slow things down. To build faster on solid ground.

If you're leading anything complex right now, borrow that question. It's uncomfortable. That's how you know it's working.

What's your "pressure test" question before you commit?
