Pursuing an MBA at 40+ while leading a $50M program.
Here's why.

Not for the credential.
Not to check a box.

Because I kept hitting the same ceiling.

I'd be in executive steering committees where the CFO was talking about capital allocation models and EBITDA impact.
I'd be in board presentations where the conversation moved from operations to strategy to M&A.
I understood the operations deeply.

But I was watching others shape decisions at a level I wanted to reach.

So I enrolled at Paris ESLSCA Business School.

The timing looked insane from the outside.
Managing 15 hospitals across 3 countries.
Leading a $50M digital transformation.
Coordinating 3 EMR system rollouts simultaneously.

And starting an MBA.

Here's what I've learned so far:

**The MBA isn't adding knowledge. It's adding language.**

I now frame problems differently in executive conversations.
I connect operational decisions to capital decisions faster.
I argue for technology investments using financial modeling, not just technical rationale.

The same insight. Said differently. Heard differently.

**Second: the cohort matters more than the curriculum.**

I'm learning alongside executives from banking, logistics, energy, and real estate across the MENA region.
The cross-sector thinking alone is worth it.
Healthcare IT has blind spots. Every sector does.
The MBA is fixing mine.

**Third: age is an advantage in an MBA, not a liability.**

When case studies come up about organizational change or scaling operations, I have context that 28-year-olds in the room don't.

That's not arrogance. That's just how it works.

If you're 40+ and wondering whether an executive MBA still makes sense, my honest answer is: the later you do it, the more value you extract from it.
