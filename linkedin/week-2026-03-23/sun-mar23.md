The GCC just committed $100B+ to digital transformation.
Most of it will be wasted.

Not because the ambition is wrong.
Because the execution model is.

**The Problem**

Saudi Vision 2030, UAE's We the UAE 2031, Qatar National Vision 2030.

These are serious commitments backed by serious capital.

And across every sector, from healthcare to education to logistics, the money is flowing.

But here's what the headlines don't say:

The majority of large-scale digital transformation programs fail to deliver their promised outcomes.

Not my opinion.
Industry data. Consistently, across every market, every decade.

And the GCC is not immune.

**Why Most of It Gets Wasted**

Three patterns I've seen repeat across sectors:

**1. Technology first, strategy last.**

The vendor gets selected before the operating model is designed.
The platform gets built before anyone has agreed on what problem it's solving.
You end up with world-class software running on first-generation processes.

**2. No one owns the outcome.**

There's a project sponsor. There's a PMO. There's a steering committee.
Nobody is accountable for whether it actually changed anything.
The project delivers. The transformation doesn't.

**3. Change management is treated as training.**

"We'll run a 2-day workshop before go-live."
That's not change management. That's notification.

Real change management starts on day one and runs through year two.

**What Works**

I've managed $50M in digital transformation across 15 hospitals and 3 countries.
The programs that deliver share three things: an accountable executive, a defined outcome metric, and a change management function with real authority.

The GCC has the capital.
The question is whether it has the execution discipline.
