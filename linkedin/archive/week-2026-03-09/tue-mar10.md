Health Catalyst documented $1.5 billion in validated outcomes.
Here's what that number means when you're the one implementing it.

It means Texas Children's Hospital reduced hospital-acquired conditions by 35%.
It means Thibodaux Regional added $1.2M in revenue in a single year.

Those aren't marketing slides.
Those are outcomes from hospitals that did the hard work.

**I'm now on the implementation side of that number.**

When we signed Health Catalyst into our network, I knew the case studies.
What I didn't know was what it takes to actually get there.

Here's what nobody puts in the press release:

**The gap between "platform live" and "outcomes realized" is where transformations die.**

Month 1: Everyone is excited.
Month 3: The data is messier than expected.
Month 6: Stakeholders start asking when the ROI kicks in.
Month 9: You're rebuilding workflows nobody documented.

The hospitals in those $1.5B of validated outcomes didn't succeed because they bought great software.
They succeeded because someone stayed in the room long enough to fix the hard things.

What I've learned managing this across 15 hospitals:

1. Start with one outcome metric everyone agrees on. Just one.
2. Map the data gap before you map the workflow. You can't fix what you can't see.
3. The clinical champion matters more than the CIO. Win the floor, not the boardroom.
4. Plan for 18 months, not 90 days. Any vendor who says 90 days hasn't done this at scale.
5. Celebrate small wins loudly. The team needs to see progress before they believe in the destination.

The $1.5B number is real.
But it's made of thousands of small decisions made correctly.
