4.6 million care gaps closed.
Here's what one care gap looks like in a real hospital.

Not the aggregate. The individual.

A care gap is a 58-year-old diabetic patient who was discharged six months ago.
He was supposed to come back in 90 days for an HbA1c check.
He never came back.

Nobody called him.
Nobody flagged the missed appointment.
Nobody connected the discharge record to the follow-up protocol.

Six months later, he's back. In the emergency department.
Avoidable complication. Longer stay. Higher cost. Worse outcome.

**That's one care gap.**

Multiply that by thousands of patients across 15 hospitals.
Most hospital systems have no mechanism to even know this happened.

**What Health Catalyst's platform does:**

It surfaces these patients before the complication.
It identifies the gap between what the care protocol says should happen and what actually happened.
It puts a name on a screen so that someone can make a phone call.

4.6 million care gaps closed doesn't mean 4.6 million people got a new treatment.

It means 4.6 million times, the right person was contacted at the right time.

That's the transformation.

**What I've learned implementing this:**

The technology is 20% of the work.
The other 80% is building the workflow that makes sure someone acts on what the system surfaces.

You need a care coordinator who checks the dashboard.
You need a protocol that defines who calls and when.
You need a feedback loop so you know if the outreach worked.

Technology surfaces the problem.
The transformation executive builds the system that fixes it.

That's the job.
