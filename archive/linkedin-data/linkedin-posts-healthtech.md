## POST 2: EMR Implementation ROI

**Most hospitals get EMR implementation wrong.**

They focus on technology.
Not on change management.

Result: $50M systems with 40% utilization rates.

Here's what actually works:

**1. Start with workflows, not features**
Map your current state first.
Identify 5 pain points.
Pick 1-2 AI automations to solve them.

**2. Executive sponsorship isn't optional**
Your CMO and CFO need to co-own this.
Not IT alone.

**3. Training is ongoing, not one-time**
Plan for 6 months of adoption support.
Budget for it.

**4. Measure utilization, not just deployment**
Track: How many doctors actually use it?
Not: How many licenses are deployed?

**5. Plan for the 3rd month dip**
Usage always drops in month 3.
Have a recovery plan ready.

The ROI is real:
• 30% reduction in documentation time
• 25% improvement in coding accuracy
• 50% faster discharge processes

But only if you execute the change, not just the technology.

What's your EMR implementation lesson?

## POST 5: Why I Turned Down a 6-Figure Salary

**Sometimes the best career move is saying no.**

I was offered a Chief Digital Officer role at a well-known hospital group.

6-figure salary. Impressive title. Great benefits.

I turned it down.

Here's why:

1. **It wasn't a transformation role.**
They wanted someone to "manage vendors" and "keep systems running."
Not someone to drive change.

2. **The comp was actually a pay cut.**
Base salary looked good.
But my consulting rate × days worked = 3x the guaranteed comp.
I was trading upside for downside protection I didn't need.

3. **Geography was limiting.**
On-site only. No flexibility.
I could reach more hospitals, patients, impact as an advisor.

4. **Timing was wrong.**
They needed someone yesterday.
I was in the middle of a transformation at SGH.
Finishing matters more than starting.

The lesson:

Before accepting any executive role, ask:

- Is this actually a transformation seat, or a maintenance seat?
- Is the comp structured for risk or comfort?
- What's the flexibility?
- Can I finish what I start?

I chose to stay.
Build ANCO.
And help hospitals transform on my terms.

When did you say no to an opportunity that wasn't right?
