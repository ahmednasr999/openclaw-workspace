# SGH HIS Adoption Readiness and Change Risk Report, Version 2
Date: 2026-03-02
Prepared for: PMO and Program Leadership
Scope: HIS adoption readiness, change risks, and go-live mitigation controls

## 1) Executive Summary
Current readiness is at risk due to unresolved adoption gaps across training, ownership clarity, workflow alignment, engagement, and capability readiness. Without immediate mitigation ownership and gate-based control, go-live stability is exposed to elevated risk.

Decision intent:
- Do not proceed with go-live on assumptions.
- Proceed only against measurable readiness gates.

## 2) Method and Evidence Basis
Assessment approach:
- Structured observation during configured patient journey validation sessions.
- 5-point behavioral scoring model (1 to 5).
- ADKAR-aligned dimensions:
  - Workflow Acceptance
  - Ownership and Accountability
  - Standardization Commitment
  - Adoption Readiness Signals
  - Resistance Risk Signals (reverse)
  - Training and Go-Live Impact
  - Process Governance and Role Clarity

Evidence requirement for final sign-off:
- Attach dimension-level score table by site and function.
- Attach variance analysis and critical outlier list.

## 4) Readiness Gate Model (Go or No-Go)
Go-live gate must pass all critical controls:

Gate G1, Super-user readiness:
- 90 percent of designated super-users complete scenario demonstration and support simulation.

Gate G2, Workflow ownership clarity:
- 100 percent of critical workflows have named owner, backup owner, and escalation authority.

Gate G3, Alignment closure:
- Zero unresolved critical scenario disputes.

Gate G4, Capability readiness:
- Minimum 85 percent end-user readiness score before training wave completion.

Gate G5, Resistance control:
- High-risk resistance groups have mitigation plans, owners, and weekly follow-up evidence.

Any failed critical gate = no-go until corrective actions are closed.

## 5) Two-Week Mitigation Sprint Plan
Week 1:
- Finalize RACI and ownership communication.
- Run super-user readiness validation clinics.
- Close unresolved workflow scenarios.
- Publish high-risk resistance list and interventions.

Week 2:
- Execute targeted remediation training.
- Validate gate metrics and evidence packs.
- Conduct mock go-live simulation for high-risk workflows.
- Present gate closure status to steering committee.

## 6) Weekly KPI Dashboard (Adoption Control)
Track weekly at PMO forum:
- Super-user readiness completion percent
- Critical workflow ownership completeness percent
- Unresolved scenario count
- End-user readiness percent by function
- Resistance incidents opened and closed
- High-severity escalation aging

## 7) Governance and Reporting Cadence
- Daily operational check-in: Change Lead, Training Lead, PMO
- Twice-weekly risk review: Program leadership
- Weekly steering report: gate status, residual risk, and go-live recommendation

## 8) Recommended Decision
Proceed with controlled readiness sprint immediately.
Do not lock final go-live until all critical gates are met with evidence.
