# Ahmed LinkedIn Content Policy

Updated: 2026-04-08
Owner: NASR

## Purpose
Build executive authority that compounds into opportunities.

This policy does not optimize for vanity virality. It optimizes for:
- useful insight
- executive credibility
- relevance to GCC transformation and AI adoption
- repeatable publishing discipline

## Positioning
Ahmed is positioned as:
- a senior transformation executive
- an operator who has led scale in complex environments
- a practical AI adoption voice, not an AI hype merchant
- a GCC-aware leader who understands regulated, high-stakes execution

## Core Rule
Every post must help the reader do one of these:
- make a better decision
- avoid a common mistake
- understand a pattern earlier
- apply a practical framework
- reframe a transformation or AI problem clearly

If the post is mainly self-expression, it does not ship.

## What We Want More Of
- reader-first framing
- operator lessons from real work
- specific proof points and metrics
- practical frameworks and models
- informed contrarian takes
- visuals that feel useful, not promotional
- clear calls to think, respond, or share experience

## What We Want Less Of
- generic motivation
- vague thought leadership
- bragging dressed up as insight
- cheap outrage hooks
- ad-looking branded visuals
- jargon without meaning
- personal stories that do not teach anything

## Strategic Filters
Before drafting, define these four things:
1. Reader: who is this for?
2. Tension: what real problem or wrong belief are we addressing?
3. Lesson: what is the one thing they should walk away with?
4. Proof: what real experience, metric, or observation makes this credible?

## Content Pillars
1. AI adoption in enterprise
2. Transformation in regulated industries
3. PMO and execution discipline
4. Leadership and operating model decisions
5. GCC market realities and opportunities
6. Scaling systems, teams, and cross-functional execution
7. Career-proof lessons from real transformation work

## Post Types
Use these intentionally.

### 1. Framework post
Best for reach and saves.
Examples:
- maturity model
- checklist
- decision tree
- comparison framework

### 2. Operator lesson
Best for authority.
Structure: moment -> mistake/tension -> lesson -> application.

### 3. Contrarian take
Best for engagement.
Must be grounded in evidence, not provocation for its own sake.

### 4. Market signal post
Best for relevance.
Tie a current trend to executive action.

### 5. Personal proof post
Best for trust.
Use only when the story proves something useful for the reader.

## Hooks Policy
The first 2 lines must create one of:
- tension
- contradiction
- consequence
- curiosity
- specificity

Allowed:
- "Most AI programs fail before the model does."
- "The PMO is not slowing you down. Your operating model is."

Not allowed:
- performative insults
- hustle-bro bait
- fake controversy
- empty shock lines

## Body Policy
A post body should do this:
- name the problem fast
- explain why it happens
- give a usable lens or framework
- prove it with real experience or a concrete observation
- end with a discussion-worthy close

## Personal Story Rule
Personal stories are allowed only if they do one of these:
- prove the point
- humanize the tension
- show what changed your thinking
- reveal a scar, tradeoff, or operational lesson

Do not post life updates unless they create real value for the audience.

## Media Policy
Preferred formats:
- 1080x1350 single-image frameworks
- clean infographics
- checklists
- comparison visuals
- operating-system diagrams
- annotated models

Visual standard:
- useful first
- clean second
- branded third

The visual should feel like something worth saving or forwarding.
It should not look like an ad.
It should also not look amateurish.

## Cadence
Minimum viable cadence:
- 3 strong posts per week

Target cadence:
- 5 posts per week

Recommended weekly mix:
- 2 framework or tactical posts
- 1 contrarian or market insight post
- 1 operator lesson post
- 1 personal-proof or leadership post

## Commenting Rule
Comments are part of the strategy, not an afterthought.

For every published post:
- reply thoughtfully to meaningful comments
- add at least 3-5 strong comments on relevant external posts each week
- use comments to sharpen positioning and build relationships

## Quality Gate
Score each draft from 1-5 on:
- usefulness
- clarity
- hook strength
- executive signal
- save/share potential
- authenticity

If any category is below 4, revise.

## Approval Rule
Nothing publishes without Ahmed approval.
Drafts move to review first.

## Success Metrics
Primary:
- qualified profile views
- high-quality comments
- inbound conversations
- relevant connection requests
- opportunity-related DMs

Secondary:
- saves
- shares
- follower growth
- impressions

Follower growth matters, but it is not the top KPI.
Authority and opportunity quality matter more.

## Working Principle
AI gives leverage.
Judgment gives authority.
Consistency gives compounding.
